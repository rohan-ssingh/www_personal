Bio

I am majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Religion at Columbia Engineering. I am an Egleston Scholar, awarded to the top 1% of Columbia Engineering applicants (Link). My research interests lie at the intersection of artificial intelligence, software systems, healthcare technologies, and cybersecurity, with a focus on developing innovative solutions such as AI-powered medical devices, robust chatbot evaluation tools, and privacy-enhancing browser extensions.

My past research experiences have spanned natural language processing, computer vision, neuroimaging, computational genomics, and protein structure prediction. I have conducted research at:

  • the Dynamic Perception and Memory Lab at Columbia, under Prof. Chris Baldassano and Jiawen Huang;
  • the AlQuraishi Lab at Columbia under Prof. Mohammed AlQuraishi and Dr. Nazim Bouatta;
  • the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Lab, under Dr. Vince Pulido and Dr. Benjamin Skerritt-Davis;
  • the Schrauwen Lab at Columbia Medical Center, under Prof. Isabelle Schrauwen;
  • the Quantitative Imaging and AI Lab at Stanford, under Prof. Daniel Rubin and Dr. Hersh Sagreiya.
I spent my most recent summer interning at PayPal as an AI Engineer Intern.
